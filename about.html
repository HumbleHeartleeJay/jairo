De La Salle John Bosco College

A PAASCU-accredited Lasallian district school in Mangagoy, Bislig City, Surigao del Sur — offering pre-school, basic education, senior high school, college, and TESDA-accredited TVET programs.

Who We Are

Rooted in faith, built for service

DLSJBC traces its founding to 1963, when the Salesian Fathers of Don Bosco agreed to manage a new boys' school in Mangagoy. In 1977, its administration passed to the De La Salle Brothers, and in 2007 the school formally became the 18th district school of De La Salle Philippines under its present name.

Today the college carries forward that Lasallian and Salesian heritage — forming students from Nursery through college with programs that include TESDA-accredited technical-vocational courses.

Our Story

A timeline of milestones

From a small elementary school for company dependents to a full Lasallian district college — here's how DLSJBC took shape, summarized in brief.

  1. Mid-1950s

    A company school takes root

    What became DLSJBC began as a small elementary school serving the children of employees of the Bislig Bay Lumber Company, forerunner of the paper manufacturer PICOP.

  2. 1961–63

    From company school to parish, then Salesian care

    Management passed to the local parish priest, opening enrollment beyond company families. Soon after, a school for boys was proposed for Mangagoy, and the Salesian Fathers of Don Bosco agreed to run it, with backing from a co-owner of the lumber company.

  3. 1964–69

    St. John Bosco Technical High School

    Teachers from Don Bosco Mandaluyong formed the pioneering staff. Over the decade the boys' school and a neighboring girls' school eventually merged into one co-educational institution, later renamed John Bosco School and moved to its present site on La Salle Drive.

  4. 1974–1982

    A second campus, then a merger

    A sister campus opened in nearby Coleto to serve additional company families. By 1982, that campus and the Mangagoy school were brought together under one administration, still known as John Bosco School.

  5. 1977

    Lasallian administration begins

    As the Salesian Fathers gradually stepped back, oversight of the school was formally handed to the De La Salle Brothers — the start of its Lasallian identity.

  6. 1997

    Consolidating operations

    After the lumber company withdrew its financial support, the Coleto campus's operations were folded into the main campus, which by then had also added a nursery level.

  7. 1999–2007

    College department opens; district-school status

    John Bosco School became John Bosco College with a new College Department. Accreditation followed through La Salle's schools supervision office, and in 2007 the institution was formally welcomed as a district school of De La Salle Philippines — taking on its present name, De La Salle John Bosco College.

Today

A complete Lasallian education, K–12 and beyond

DLSJBC now offers complete pre-school, grade school, junior and senior high school, college, and TESDA-accredited technical-vocational programs — continuing a legacy of faith and service in Bislig City.
